## Getting Started

### What is Unciv Soccer?

This mod replaces all Civilization VI concepts with football/soccer themes:
- **Cities** = Football Clubs
- **Settlers** = Club Chairmen (found new clubs)
- **Workers** = Groundstaff (build pitches and facilities)
- **Military Units** = Players and Firm Members (compete for control)
- **Gold** = Transfer Kitty (money for transfers and facilities)
- **Science** = Tactical Knowledge (research new formations and strategies)
- **Culture** = Legendary Status (grow your club's fame)
- **Faith** = Fan Passion (supporter engagement)
- **Happiness** = Board Confidence (management approval)

### Starting a New Game

1. Select **Unciv Soccer** as your ruleset
2. Choose a **Difficulty Level** (Park Sunday to Super League Elite)
3. Choose a **Game Speed** (Full Time Marathon to Penalty Shootout Blitz)
4. Select your **Club** (from 93 different football clubs worldwide)
5. Click **Start Game**

You'll begin with:
- **1 Chairman** (settler unit) - can found new cities
- **1 Groundstaff** (worker unit) - can build improvements
- **0-1 starting military units** (depending on difficulty/era)

*Note: We've reduced the starting unit count from 4 to optimize early gameplay!*

## Game Basics

### Cities (Clubs)

Each city represents a football club. Cities produce:
- **Production** → Build units, buildings, and improvements
- **Transfer Kitty (Gold)** → Purchase units and buildings
- **Tactical Knowledge (Science)** → Research new technologies
- **Legendary Status (Culture)** → Expand borders and unlock policies
- **Fan Passion (Faith)** → Generate Great People

### Board Confidence (Happiness)

Keep your Board Confidence above **-10** or face serious penalties:
- **High Confidence** (above 5): Faster growth and production
- **Neutral** (0-5): Normal gameplay
- **Low Confidence** (-1 to -10): Slow growth, reduced production, units weaker
- **Crisis** (below -10): Growth stops, can't build settlers, may lose control

Boost Board Confidence by:
- Building entertainment and culture buildings
- Winning battles
- Avoiding losing too much territory

### Combat System

When you attack rivals:
1. Position your military units adjacent to enemy tiles
2. Right-click to attack
3. Combat strength = Unit's Strength value
4. Bonus/Penalty based on terrain, unit types, and tactics
5. Win = Take control of the rival's tile

## Units Guide

### Civilian Units

**Chairman** (Settler)
- Founds new cities/clubs
- Only produces 1 per city
- Can only be used once
- Cost: 80 Production
- Essential for expanding your league

**Groundstaff** (Worker)
- Builds improvements on tiles (Pitch, Training Ground, Car Park, etc.)
- Can clear terrain and terraform
- Can be automated to work automatically
- Cost: 50 Production
- Requires: Sunday League Tech

**The Physio** (Medic)
- Heals all adjacent Military units (+15 HP healing to others, +15 to self)
- Essential for keeping your army healthy during long campaigns
- Cost: 110 Production
- Requires: Sports Science Tech

### Early Combat Units (Eras 1-2)

**Youth Prospect** (Scout)
- Fast reconnaissance unit for exploring the map
- Movement: 3 | Strength: 5
- Available from the start
- Good for early exploration and scouting enemy positions

**Car Park Hardman** (Warrior)
- Basic melee combat unit
- Movement: 2 | Strength: 10
- Available from: Sunday League Tech
- Good for early defense and aggression

**Coach Voyager** (Scout Variant)
- Faster scout with ability to pillage
- Movement: 4 | Strength: 6
- Can destroy rival improvements outside your territory
- Requires: Away Days Tech

### Mid-Game Combat Units (Eras 2-3)

**Stone Island Shithouse** (Pikeman)
- Heavy melee unit for mid-game battles
- Movement: 2 | Strength: 20
- More durable than Warriors
- Requires: Away Days Tech

**Set-Piece Specialist** (Archer)
- Ranged unit that attacks from 2+ tiles away
- Movement: 2 | Strength: 8 | Ranged Strength: 14
- Good for siege and ranged support
- Requires: Offside Trap Tech

**Sweeper Keeper** (Knight)
- Heavy defensive unit with +33% Strength when defending
- Movement: 2 | Strength: 26
- Excellent for holding positions and fortifications
- Requires: Sweeper Keeper School Tech

### Late-Game Combat Units (Eras 3-4)

**Firm Leader** (Samurai)
- Elite melee unit with superior combat strength
- Movement: 2 | Strength: 35
- One of the strongest single combat units
- Requires: Firm Organization Tech

**Free-Kick Specialist** (Archer Variant)
- Extended-range archer (+1 Range)
- Movement: 2 | Strength: 10 | Ranged Strength: 16
- Attacks from even further than normal archers
- Requires: Terrace Chants Tech

**Ultra Capo** (General)
- Elite leader providing +15% Strength bonus to nearby Military units (2-tile range)
- Movement: 2 | Strength: 52
- Best used supporting other units
- Requires: Ultra Coordination Tech

**Firm Capo** (General Commander)
- Provides +25% Strength bonus to nearby Military units (2-tile range)
- Cannot attack directly (support only)
- Requires: The Firm Tech

### Special Units

**Laptop Guru** (Spy)
- Steals 250 Science from enemy civilizations
- Can reveal enemy positions
- One-time use
- Cost: 200 Production
- Requires: The Laptop Guru Tech

**The Pundit** (Archaeologist)
- Generates Culture (Legendary Status)
- Support unit for cultural victory
- Requires: Broadcast Rights Tech

### Great People

**Club Legend** (Great Artist)
- Triggers 8-turn Golden Age
- Generates culture
- Can create Landmark improvements

**Master Tactician** (Great Scientist)
- Hurries technology research
- Boosts Tactical Knowledge
- Can create Academy improvements

**Director of Football** (Great Merchant)
- Generates Trade Routes
- Can trade with City-States
- Creates Customs House improvements

## Tips & Strategy

### Early Game (Sunday League - Non-League Era)

1. **Expand Quickly** - Send your Chairman to found new cities early
2. **Scout Thoroughly** - Use Youth Prospects to map the world
3. **Build Workers** - You need Groundstaff to build improvements
4. **Research Strategically** - Prioritize techs that unlock units and buildings
5. **Keep Board Confident** - Avoid losing to maintain city growth

### Mid-Game (Football League - Premier Era)

1. **Build a Strong Army** - Upgrade from Warriors to Pikemen
2. **Secure Resources** - Control strategic resource tiles
3. **Diversify Economy** - Build multiple types of income buildings
4. **Establish Trade** - Build trade routes with other clubs
5. **Adapt Your Strategy** - Respond to neighbors' tactics

### Late-Game (Super League Era)

1. **Focus on Victory Type** - Commit to domination, science, or culture
2. **Build Wonders** - Race to build powerful wonder buildings
3. **Recruit Great People** - Get Artists, Scientists, and Merchants
4. **Coordinate Units** - Use Generals to support your army
5. **Balance Economy** - Maintain growth while building toward victory

### General Tips

- **Balance is Key** - Don't neglect any resource type
- **Diplomacy Matters** - Ally with stronger neighbors when needed
- **Defense First** - Garrison units in cities to prevent takeover
- **City Planning** - Place units strategically for maximum coverage
- **Tech Diversity** - Research a mix of military, economic, and cultural techs
- **Watch Your Happiness** - Negative Board Confidence cripples your club
- **Use Great People Wisely** - They're one-time bonuses; save for critical moments

## FAQ

**Q: Why do I start with fewer units?**  
A: We've optimized the starting experience! Now you begin with just a Chairman (founder) and minimal units, letting you grow strategically.

**Q: How do I make money?**  
A: Build Revenue-generating buildings like Club Shop, Local Boozer, and Broadcasting Studio. Trade with other clubs using Trade Routes. Expand your territory.

**Q: What's the best unit?**  
A: Late-game units are strongest (Ultra Capo, Firm Leader), but early units (Warriors) are cost-effective. Use the right unit for each situation.

**Q: How do I increase culture?**  
A: Build cultural buildings, recruit Great Artists, establish trade routes, and research cultural techs.

**Q: Can I lose?**  
A: Yes! Keep Board Confidence above -10 or your club faces collapse. You can also be conquered militarily.

**Q: How long do games take?**  
A: Varies by Game Speed. Marathon (2x costs) = 500+ turns. Standard = 300 turns. Quick = 200 turns. Blitz = 100 turns.
